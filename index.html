Who I Am

More than one thing

I don't fit neatly into one box. And that's the point.

I didn't start coding to get a job. I started because I watched my family run a manufacturing business on paper: inventory in registers, orders managed by memory. I knew I could build something better. That became BLP Inventory, a full ERP system I built completely alone, that a real business actually runs on today. That single project taught me something I carry into everything I do: the best work comes from genuinely understanding the problem from the inside, not just writing code at it.

From there, curiosity took me everywhere. Into client meetings at software companies, learning to listen before I document anything. Into financial models where I valued Infosys four different ways. Into Python notebooks building ML models for government infrastructure. Into Google Ads dashboards doubling a restaurant's revenue from Vietnam. I don't stay in one lane. I've completely stopped apologising for it.

Right now, what has me completely obsessed is AI. Not just using it, but building with it, talking to it, understanding it at a character level. I spend hours crafting prompts, designing personality systems, exploring what language models can do when you give them the right context and genuine intent. Saathi, my AI companion rooted in Bhagavad Gita wisdom, was born from a question that kept me up at night: what if someone struggling emotionally could talk to something that truly understood Indian pain, in their own language, without judgement? I'm building that. Alone. From scratch. Because nobody else was.

I'm not easy to categorise. That's exactly the point.
